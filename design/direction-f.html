5 directions · camera-first angle · private people-media
The camera-app angle: the camera IS home — not a mode inside home. Capture is one tap, face-tagging is silent, the "roll" replaces the feed, memories develop like film. The app should feel like a camera with a built-in journal, not a journal with a camera button. Every palette below renders the same two screens — the camera-first home, and one moment card — so you can compare the feel, not just the hex values.
1 · Aurora
Social energy · current, refined
The current direction, tightened. Aurora gradient (violet→magenta) is the brand. Dark void base with radial glows so it's never flat black. Feels social, energetic, modern — like a nightlife moment. Best if the app leans into the "social energy" of seeing people often.

2 · Film Lab
Analog archive · contact-sheet aesthetic
Warm charcoal base, cream text, burnt orange + faded gold. The camera-app angle gets literal: moments are "exposures," the roll is a contact sheet, memories "develop" on a 24h delay (Dispo-style). Serif headers + monospace dates. No gradients — flat warm tones, thin borders, paper-grain texture. Feels like a darkroom journal. Best for the "private archive" soul of the app.

3 · Midnight Ink
Quiet observatory · introspective
Deep indigo-black, silver text, a single electric cyan accent used sparingly. No gradients, no glows, no scan animations. Sharp 4px corners. Thin 1px lines. Generous whitespace. How to choose
The camera-app angle works in all five, but the soul is different. Pick based on what feeling you want when the user opens the app 12 times a day.
Aurora
Pick if the app feels social, energetic, nightlife. "I see people often and I want the app to feel alive." Gradient brand, glowing accents, modern.
Film Lab
Pick if the app feels like a private archive / darkroom. "Moments are exposures. Some develop later." Analog, warm, archival. Dispo-style delayed memories.
Midnight Ink
Pick if the app feels introspective, quiet. "I want to notice drift without the app nagging me." Single accent, no gradients, no animation. A logbook.
Sunbleached
Pick if the app is a daytime diary. "I log lunches and coffees, not nights out." Light mode, cream paper, coral ink, Polaroid feel. Friendliest.
Neon Darkroom
Pick if the app is a camera instrument first. "Just capture, tag, and get out of my way." Pure black OLED, lime HUD, monospace, zero sentiment. Most "camera-app" of the five.
My recommendation for the camera-app angle: Film Lab. The "exposures develop on a delay" metaphor is the strongest engagement hook — it gives people a reason to come back 24h later (your memory is ready). It leans into the private-archive soul from MOMENTS_PLAN.md (no sharing, personal graph, recaps). The analog aesthetic also differentiates from every other dark-mode social app. Aurora is the safe bet if you want modern/familiar. Neon Darkroom if you want to go full camera-tool and shed the journal skin entirely.
